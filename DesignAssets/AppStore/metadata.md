## Description (max 4,000 characters)

What happened while you were away?

Your Mac already knows: the screen unlocked, the machine woke, apps launched, files changed. MacAlarm turns those signals into a readable, trustworthy history — a live timeline you can scroll, filter, and search.

A LIVE SECURITY TIMELINE
MacAlarm's background recorder captures meaningful local events the moment they happen: screen lock and unlock, sleep and wake, app launches and focus changes, changes to files you choose to watch, and recorder health heartbeats. The timeline shows them left to right, with filters to prioritize what matters and an inspector for the details of every event.

TAMPER-EVIDENT BY CONSTRUCTION
Every event is written to an append-only ledger where each record is cryptographically chained to the one before it (HMAC-SHA256). If anything is altered, deleted, or reordered, verification fails and MacAlarm tells you. The chain head is also anchored to your iCloud Drive, so even truncating the ledger is detectable. You can export a verifiable proof bundle at any time.

PRIVATE BY DESIGN
Everything stays on your Mac. No account, no cloud service, no analytics. MacAlarm never logs keystrokes, never takes screenshots, never records your microphone, and never reads your messages or browsing. The recorder is a visible login item you install, see, and remove yourself — consent first, always.

BUILT FOR TINKERERS
Your own scripts and tools can add events to the timeline through a simple command-line interface — backup jobs, audio detectors, custom security checks, anything. MacAlarm is written in pure Swift and is fully open source under the MIT license, so you can audit every line.

WHAT IT RECORDS
• Screen lock and unlock
• Sleep, wake, and display events
• App launch, activation, and termination
• Changes to files and folders you choose
• Your own custom events via CLI
• Recorder health and ledger integrity

Requires macOS 14 or later.
